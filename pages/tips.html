💡 Bali Travel Tips

Essential advice to make your trip smooth and enjoyable.

Best Time to Visit

April to October offers sunny weather and fewer rains — perfect for beaches and outdoor adventures.

Currency & Payments

Carry Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). ATMs are common, but keep cash for local shops and markets.

Transport Options

Scooters are popular for short trips. For longer journeys, taxis and private drivers are reliable.

Temple Etiquette

Wear modest clothing and sarongs when visiting temples. Respect local customs and ceremonies.

Safety & Health

Stay hydrated, use sunscreen, and be cautious with street food. Always keep travel insurance handy.

Food & Dining

Try local dishes like Nasi Goreng and Satay. Stick to busy restaurants for hygiene and authentic flavors.

Language Basics

Learning a few Indonesian phrases like “Terima Kasih” (Thank you) goes a long way in connecting with locals.

Connectivity

Buy a local SIM card for affordable data. Wi‑Fi is common in hotels and cafes, but mobile data is more reliable.

Weather Prep

Pack light cotton clothes for the heat, but also carry a raincoat or umbrella during monsoon months.

Shopping Tips

Bargaining is common in local markets. Start low, smile, and enjoy the experience of negotiating fair prices.
